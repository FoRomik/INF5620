Abstract problem setting

Consider an abstract differential equation

$$ \mathcal{L}(u)=0\ts$$ Example: \( \mathcal{L}(u)=u'(t)+a(t)u(t)-b(t) \).

The corresponding discrete equation:

$$ \mathcal{L}_{\Delta}(u) =0\ts$$ Let now

  • \( u \) be the numerical solution of the discrete equations

  • \( \uex \) the exact solution of the differential equation
Then

$$ \begin{align*} \mathcal{L}(\uex)&=0,\\ \mathcal{L}_\Delta(u)&=0\ts \end{align*} $$ The numerical solution is computed at mesh points: \( u^n \), \( n=0,\ldots,N_t \).
